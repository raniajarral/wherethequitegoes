Where Does the Quiet Go?

Noise pollution harms people's health and is detrimental to local animal life that depend on sound to survive. It can cause stress, hearing loss, and disrupt natural behaviors in wildlife.

To solve this, cities should enforce noise limits and design quieter transport systems. Green zones and trees can absorb sound and create peaceful buffers. Communities must also practice discipline by turning down speakers, maintaining vehicles, and respecting quiet hours.

By managing noise pollution, we protect both human wellbeing and the natural balance of our environment. Reducing noise means giving every living being the calm it needs to flourish.
